## On the GTOBot itself

The smoke test was clean: BTN with AKs opens to 6 (3bb), UTG with 72o folds, set of aces on AKQ flop value-bets ~0.6 pot (sized up for wetness), and 72o on the same flop bluffs ~30% of the time. That last one is the win — the *same* hand at the *same* spot doesn't always do the same thing. That's the predictability-killer the day-3 plan called out.

A subtle thing the build forced me to confront: the `position_label` heuristic uses engine seats (SB=0, BB=1, BTN=n-1), but with sit-outs `n_active` varies per hand. A bot in engine seat 2 is "UTG" at a 6-handed table and "MP" or "CO" at smaller tables. The charts only have UTG/MP/CO/BTN/SB/BB labels, so smaller tables collapse into the wider chart by accident — fine for v1, but a lurking source of slightly-off ranges in 4- and 5-handed play.

Also: `OpponentStats` (VPIP/PFR/AF) is built but unwired. The bot's range model uses action history directly to narrow ranges. Stats would *bias* those defaults (a calling station's "preflop call" range is wider than 40%). That layer is the obvious next iteration.

- **CFR-trained preflop charts.** The graduation step. Replace `preflop_charts.py` with a strategy table generated by MCCFR on a preflop-only abstraction. ~hours of compute, no architectural change to the bot.
- **Multi-street barrel logic.** Right now each street is a fresh decision; a real GTO bot plans turn/river barrels at flop time. Adding it would mean `GTOBot` needs to remember its own intent across streets, which the current stateless `act(obs)` interface doesn't support cleanly.
